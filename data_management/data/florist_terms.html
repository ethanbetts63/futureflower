1. Definitions & Relationship

1.1 Parties.

“FutureFlower” refers to the platform named above. “Florist” means an independent business or individual accepted to fulfill orders through the platform.

1.2 Relationship.

Florists are independent contractors. Nothing in this agreement creates employment, agency, joint venture, or partnership. Florists control their operations and are responsible for licenses, taxes, insurance and staff.

2. Scope of Services

2.1

FutureFlower provides an order-matching and payments platform that connects customers with participating florists. Florists accept orders through the platform and deliver goods/services to the customer.

3. Commission & Discounts

3.1 Commission.

Platform commission is presently 15% of the order subtotal (bouquet + add-ons). Commission is deducted before payout.

3.2 Discounts.

Any promotional discount applied to an order is taken from FutureFlower’s commission and does not reduce the florist payout or declared bouquet value. FutureFlower may offer discounts at its discretion and may change discount policy or commission rates at any time; continued use constitutes acceptance of changes.

3.3 Calculation.

Commission is calculated before tax. Payouts to florists are based on the order amount after commission (and before applicable taxes that the florist must report).

4. Order Assignment, Acceptance & Rejection

4.1 Customer-acquired orders.

If the customer was signed up or referred to FutureFlower by the florist, that florist receives the first right of refusal and has 24 hours to accept or decline the order. If the florist does not respond within 24 hours, FutureFlower may reassign the order immediately or, at its discretion, extend the response window.

4.2 Platform-assigned orders.

Orders assigned directly by FutureFlower may be assigned and/or reassigned immediately to any partner florist; those florists may accept or decline but have no priority entitlement.

4.3 Binding acceptance.

Acceptance of an order is a binding commitment to fulfill that order.

4.4 Rejection rewards & penalties.

Only orders for customers personally signed up by the florist are eligible for a rejection reward; orders not signed up by the florist are not eligible. There are no penalties for rejecting orders. Reward amounts will be tiered by order value and may be changed by FutureFlower. Abuse of the rejection system (e.g., gaming acceptance/cancellation) may lead to suspension or termination.

5. Customer Brief & Quality Standards

5.1 Customer brief.

For each order, the florist will receive the customer’s delivery details, total budget (bouquet + delivery), and stated bouquet preferences and notes.

5.2 Matching preferences.

Florists must reasonably match customer preferences. Exact replication is not required; reasonable substitutions are allowed, however unreasonable substitutions are not (e.g. customer states they are allergic to a flower, and that flower is in the bouquet). If preferences are unreasonable or cannot be met, the florist should decline the order before accepting. If a florist accepts and later discovers they cannot meet the brief, they must notify FutureFlower immediately.

5.3 Bouquet value.

Florists must supply an arrangement equivalent in value, size and presentation to what they would deliver at the same budget to a direct customer (including delivery). FutureFlower recognizes some subjectivity and will generally give reasonable deference to the florist but reserves final discretion in disputes.

5.4 Branding & reviews.

Florists are encouraged to include their branding. Customer feedback is monitored; repeated quality complaints may result in suspension.

6. Delivery Obligations & Confirmation

6.1 Delivery responsibility.

Florists are responsible for safe, timely delivery and compliance with local laws, including maintaining suitable vehicles, staff and insurance. Florists may refuse unsafe delivery tasks.

6.2 Confirmation.

Florists must confirm delivery status as soon as reasonably possible and no later than 24 hours after the scheduled delivery date using the FutureFlower system (confirmation link or dashboard). Confirmation may include optional notes and delivery time. It is advised but not necessary, that florists maintain internal proof of delivery. Repeated failure to confirm deliveries may lead to suspension.

6.3 Delivery failure.

If delivery fails, the florist must record the reason (e.g., recipient unavailable, access issue, incorrect address) and inform FutureFlower via the FutureFlower system (confirmation link or dashboard).

6.4 Payout Contingency:

Payouts are not contingent upon delivery confirmation. However, FutureFlower reserves the right to modify payout procedures in the future.

7. Refunds, Fault Allocation & Chargebacks

7.1 Who handles refunds.

All customer refunds are processed by FutureFlower. Florists must not issue refunds directly to customers.

7.2 If FutureFlower is at fault.

FutureFlower will absorb refunds arising from platform errors (payment processing duplications, system errors, incorrect information sent to florist, website/app errors).

7.3 If the florist is at fault.

Florist is at fault where delivery failed due to florist action, bouquet quality is below reasonable standards, incorrect address handling when correct information was provided, or damage caused during delivery. If a refund is required for an order already paid, the florist must repay FutureFlower. FutureFlower may deduct refunds from future payouts or reverse pending commissions; failure to reimburse may result in collection actions.

7.4 Chargebacks & disputes.

If a customer disputes a payment, FutureFlower may withhold or reverse payouts pending investigation. Payouts may be reversed where a chargeback is attributable to florist conduct or fraud.

8. Payouts & Stripe Connect — Stripe

8.1 Onboarding.

Florist must complete Stripe Connect onboarding (or other payment onboarding required by FutureFlower) before receiving payouts. Florists are responsible for maintaining accounts able to receive payments and for any bank or Stripe fees.

8.2 Timing.

Payouts are processed on a fixed schedule after order acceptance. While the intent is to payout before delivery, actual timing may vary due to Stripe or banking processing; payouts may occur before or after delivery.

8.3 Withholding.

FutureFlower may withhold payouts in suspected fraud, disputes, chargebacks, or other reasonable concerns. FutureFlower is not responsible for delays resulting from Stripe or banking processes.

8.4 Taxes.

Florists are solely responsible for reporting and paying taxes on payouts.

9. Customer Ownership & Solicitation

9.1 Customer ownership.

Customers acquired through the platform remain customers of FutureFlower. Florists may not solicit, redirect or attempt to transfer platform customers to off-platform subscriptions, ordering methods, or services.

9.2 Branding permitted.

Passive branding (logos, packaging) is allowed and encouraged, but florists must not include communications or inserts that actively encourage customers to bypass FutureFlower. Natural customer attraction due to quality, branding, or service is permitted, if not expected, — but proactive attempts to divert business are not.

10.2 Compliance.

Florists must handle data in accordance with applicable privacy laws and FutureFlower’s privacy policy and must not retain customer data longer than necessary for fulfilment and legal obligations.
