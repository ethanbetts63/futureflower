1.1 Parties.

“FutureFlower” refers to the platform named above. “Florist” means an independent business or individual accepted to fulfill orders through the platform.

Florists are independent contractors. Nothing in this agreement creates employment, agency, joint venture, or partnership. Florists control their operations and are responsible for licenses, taxes, insurance and staff.

2. Scope of Services

2.1

FutureFlower provides an order-matching and payments platform that connects customers with participating florists. Florists accept orders through the platform and deliver goods/services to the customer.

3. Payouts & Discounts

3.1 Payout amount.

Each order offer states the total payout to the florist (bouquet plus delivery). By accepting an order, the florist agrees to fulfill it for the stated amount. The stated payout is the full amount the florist receives for that order.

3.2 Discounts.

Any promotional discount applied to an order is funded by FutureFlower and does not reduce the florist payout stated on the order offer. FutureFlower may offer discounts at its discretion and may change its pricing and payout structure for future orders at any time; continued use constitutes acceptance of changes.

3.3 Taxes.

Payout amounts are stated before applicable taxes that the florist must report.

4. Order Assignment, Acceptance & Rejection

4.1 Assignment.

Orders are offered to partner florists at FutureFlower's discretion and may be reassigned to any partner florist if declined or unanswered. Florists may accept or decline any order.

4.2 Binding acceptance.

Acceptance of an order is a binding commitment to fulfill that order.

4.3 No penalties.

There are no penalties or rewards for declining orders. Declining an order has no contractual consequence.

5. Customer Brief & Quality Standards

5.1 Customer brief.

For each order, the florist will receive the customer’s delivery details, the payout amount, and the customer's stated bouquet preferences and notes.

5.2 Matching preferences.

Florists must reasonably match customer preferences with the stock available to them. Exact replication is not required; reasonable substitutions are allowed, however unreasonable substitutions are not (e.g. customer states they are allergic to a flower, and that flower is in the bouquet). If preferences are unreasonable or cannot be met, the florist should decline the order before accepting. If a florist accepts and later discovers they cannot meet the brief, they must notify FutureFlower immediately.

5.3 Bouquet value.

Florists must supply an arrangement equivalent in value, size and presentation to what they would deliver at the same price to a direct customer (including delivery). FutureFlower recognizes some subjectivity and will generally give reasonable deference to the florist but reserves final discretion in disputes.

5.4 Branding & reviews.

Florists are encouraged to include their branding. Customer feedback is monitored; repeated quality complaints may result in suspension.

6. Delivery Obligations & Confirmation

6.1 Delivery responsibility.

Florists are responsible for safe, timely delivery and compliance with local laws, including maintaining suitable vehicles, staff and insurance. Florists may refuse unsafe delivery tasks.

6.2 Confirmation.

Florists must confirm delivery status as soon as reasonably possible and no later than 24 hours after the scheduled delivery date using the FutureFlower system (confirmation link or dashboard). Confirmation may include optional notes and delivery time. It is advised but not necessary, that florists maintain internal proof of delivery. Repeated failure to confirm deliveries may lead to suspension.

6.3 Delivery failure.

If delivery fails, the florist must record the reason (e.g., recipient unavailable, access issue, incorrect address) and inform FutureFlower via the FutureFlower system (confirmation link or dashboard).

6.4 Payout Contingency:

Payouts are not contingent upon delivery confirmation. However, FutureFlower reserves the right to modify payout procedures in the future.

7. Refunds, Fault Allocation & Chargebacks

7.1 Who handles refunds.

All customer refunds are processed by FutureFlower. Florists must not issue refunds directly to customers.

7.2 If FutureFlower is at fault.

FutureFlower will absorb refunds arising from platform errors (payment processing duplications, system errors, incorrect information sent to florist, website/app errors).

7.3 If the florist is at fault.

Florist is at fault where delivery failed due to florist action, bouquet quality is below reasonable standards, incorrect address handling when correct information was provided, or damage caused during delivery. If a refund is required for an order already paid, the florist must repay FutureFlower. FutureFlower may deduct refunds from future payouts; failure to reimburse may result in collection actions.

7.4 Chargebacks & disputes.

If a customer disputes a payment, FutureFlower may withhold or reverse payouts pending investigation. Payouts may be reversed where a chargeback is attributable to florist conduct or fraud.
